Tribute to Nikola Tesla

The man who invented the 20th century

His Timeline

  • 1856 - Nikola Tesla is born on July 10 in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).
  • 1875 - Enrolls at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz to study electrical engineering.
  • 1880 - Attends the Charles-Ferdinand University in Prague.
  • 1881 - Begins working at a telephone company in Budapest, where he makes improvements to the equipment and develops his first invention, a device that amplifies sound.
  • 1884 - Arrives in New York City with a letter of recommendation to Thomas Edison. Begins working at Edison Machine Works.
  • 1887-1888 - Develops the induction motor and the alternating current (AC) electrical system. Patents the AC induction motor and transformer.
  • 1888 - Delivers a lecture on the new system of alternating current, gaining the attention of George Westinghouse, who invests in Tesla's AC system.
  • 1891 - Invents the Tesla coil, a high-frequency transformer that is fundamental to radio technology.
  • 1893 - Demonstrates wireless transmission of electrical energy through his experiments with the Tesla coil. Plays a key role in the World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago, where his AC system powers the fair.
  • 1895 - Builds the first hydroelectric power plant at Niagara Falls, using AC power to provide electricity to Buffalo, New York.
  • 1896 - Develops early X-ray imaging technology, contributing to the field of medical diagnostics.
  • 1898 - Demonstrates a remote-controlled boat at Madison Square Garden, showcasing early robotics and radio control technology.
  • 1899-1900 - Conducts experiments in Colorado Springs, investigating high-voltage, high-frequency electricity and wireless power transmission.
  • 1901-1905 - Begins work on the Wardenclyffe Tower project in Long Island, New York, an ambitious attempt to create a global wireless communication and power transmission system.
  • 1917 - The Wardenclyffe Tower project is abandoned due to lack of funding. Tesla is awarded the Edison Medal by the American Institute of Electrical Engineers, their highest honor.
  • 1931 - Tesla's 75th birthday is celebrated with a major feature article in Time magazine, recognizing his contributions to electrical engineering.
  • 1943 - Tesla dies on January 7 in New York City. Later that year, the U.S. Supreme Court rules in favor of Tesla's patents in radio technology, recognizing his foundational work in the field.
